# GREA Portal — Prototype Walkthrough & What to Look For

*A 5-minute tour of the prototype, with the questions to ask yourself on each
screen. Everything you see is **dummy data** — judge the structure, not the
records.*

---

## Before you start

1. **Log in** with the link and credentials sent by your Office Admin. (First
   time? You'll be asked to set a password.)
2. You'll land in the portal with a tab bar at the top: **Contacts**,
   **Pipeline**, **Mailing List**, plus a **Network** stats icon. Office Admins
   also see **My Office**.
3. As you explore, every page has a **"Help for this page"** link if you get
   stuck, and a **Feedback** button to flag anything.

> **Your north star on every screen:**
> *"Does this core feature work the way I'd actually use it — and is anything
> about *how* it's built getting in my way?"*
> We're refining how the proposed features work, not adding new ones.

---

## 1. Contacts — *the heart of the portal*

**What it does:** Search every office's contacts in one place. Find a
decision-maker, see which broker owns the relationship, and request a warm
intro.

**Try this:**
- Type a **contact name or account name** in the search box.
- Switch the search scope (e.g. **All Fields**, Contact Name, Account Name).
- Apply **sector / tag filters** to narrow results.
- Toggle **"Only contacts owned by more than one office"** to see where
  relationships overlap across the network.
- Open a contact and try **Request Intro** (Gmail or Email) — it drafts an
  outreach message.

**What to look for:**
- Are the **fields shown** the ones you'd need to size up a contact at a glance?
- Is anything **missing** (a field you always check before reaching out)?
- Does **cross-office consolidation** make sense — can you tell who "owns" a
  relationship?
- Are the **filters** the right ones for how you actually search?

---

## 2. Pipeline — *deals across the network*

**What it does:** Visibility into active deals and known opportunities across
all offices, with stages and a detail view.

**Try this:**
- **Search deals** by name, party, type, or assigned broker.
- Filter by **stage** to see where deals sit.
- Open a deal to view its **detail** — parties, assigned/specialty-team
  contacts, and stage history.

**What to look for:**
- Do the **deal stages** match how your office thinks about a pipeline?
- Is the **deal detail** enough to understand an opportunity, or is something
  key missing?
- Could you find a relevant deal in *another* office the way the search works
  today?

---

## 3. Mailing List — *shared community lists*

**What it does:** Replaces the scattered Excels and Google Sheets (e.g.
affordable housing, student housing) with shared, searchable lists.

**Try this:**
- Browse the lists and filter/search the entries.

**What to look for:**
- Are the lists you rely on represented here?
- Do they hold the **columns** you'd expect?
- Would having these in one place actually replace your current spreadsheet?

---

## 4. Network — *the national snapshot*

**What it does:** A quick health view of the whole network — totals and how
fresh each office's data is.

**Try this:**
- Open the **Network** view and scan **Total Contacts**, **Total Deals**,
  **Active Pipeline**, and **Active Listings**.
- Note the **"Due for update"** / data-freshness indicators per office.

**What to look for:**
- Is **data freshness** shown in a way you'd trust? (When real data flows, this
  is how you'll know if a record is current.)
- Are these the **right top-line numbers** to gauge the network at a glance?

---

## 5. (Office Admins only) My Office

**What it does:** View and **export** your office's contacts and deals, and
**import** data via the standard template.

**What to look for:**
- Does the **export/import** match the GREA Standard Data Format you'll be
  feeding in during Phase 2?
- Is anything about the admin view unclear?

---

## How to report what you find

- **A specific contact or deal is wrong/missing something** → click
  **"Report an issue"** on that item.
- **Everything else** (missing field, confusing layout, general note) → click
  the **Feedback** button (short title + description).

**Please don't email, text, or call feedback in** — the in-app tools keep it
all tracked. See *How Phase 1 Feedback Works* for the full process and
deadline.

---

### Remember
- It's **dummy data** — don't react to the names or numbers.
- Phase 1 is about getting the **core features right** — refining *how* they
  work, not adding new ones or touching the data/upload process (those are
  later phases).
- The goal is simple: **confirm the proposed functionality works the way you'd
  actually use it.**
